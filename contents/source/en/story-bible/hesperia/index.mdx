### Amazon Commonwealth
**Type:** Neutral Congress-administered territory  
**Territory:** The Amazon Basin — shared boundary zone for multiple surrounding polities  
**Status:** Protected under the Amazonian Treaty. No country administers it. No autonomous military presence permitted. Scientific, humanitarian, and ecological missions operate under Congress mandate.  
**Political significance:** The reason the Hesperian Congress exists. The most Myco-Net-dense territory on the continent. Every renewal of the Amazonian Treaty restructures the power balance of South Hesperian politics. The Chaco Kingdom, Inca Union, Muisca Confederation, Surinam Republic, and Atlantic Federation all border it and all have claims on record.

---

### Inca Union
**Type:** Federal state (evolved from historical Tawantinsuyu)  
**Territory:** Maximum extent of the historical Inca Empire plus additional territory, reaching to the Amazon Commonwealth border. Covers the Andes from southern Colombia through Ecuador, Peru, Bolivia, and into northern Chile and Argentina.  
**Myco-Net character:** Among the most sophisticated Andean integration stations on the continent. High-altitude, semi-independent consensus rhythms. The oldest continuously governed polity in South Hesperia.  
**Political character:** Proud of institutional continuity that predates the Congress itself. Believes its historical relationship with Amazonian peoples gives it the strongest moral claim to Commonwealth territory. The most dangerous party if the Treaty lapses. Does not raise its voice in Congress often — but when it does, other polities listen.

---

### Muisca Confederation
**Type:** Cooperative federation  
**Territory:** Current-day Colombia and western Venezuela, bordered by the Amazon Commonwealth to the south and Surinam Republic to the east.  
**Myco-Net character:** Well-integrated Andean and inter-Andean valley networks, connecting to both the Inca Union's southern substrate and the lowland networks of the Surinam Republic.  
**Political character:** The Muisca were historically sophisticated — complex goldworking, trade networks, and political organization. Given time and Myco-Net support, they consolidated what the Spanish disrupted before it could form. They share a long, layered border with the Inca Union through Ecuador and southern Colombia: two old, proud, well-organized polities that have been neighbors for centuries. Not hostile — but never simple.

---

### Surinam Republic
**Type:** Parliamentary republic  
**Territory:** Roughly the Guiana region — covering current-day Guyana, Suriname, French Guiana, and the Amazon River delta. Bordered by the Muisca Confederation to the west, the Amazon Commonwealth to the south, and the Atlantic coast to the north and east.  
**Origin:** The Dutch did not attempt conquest — they began trading until the Surinamese peoples integrated with them on their own terms. The republican tradition imported from the Dutch was adopted and transformed into something genuinely hybrid.  
**Myco-Net character:** The Guiana highlands contain some of the most ancient foundational Myco-Net substrate on the continent. Deep integration gives the Surinam Republic outsized influence relative to its geographic size.  
**Political character:** The most institutionally legible state in South Hesperia to outside observers, while being internally something quite distinct from any European model. Major trade hub. Active and sophisticated Congress participant. Its northern coast borders the Caribbean, connecting it to the Antillean Federation — a relationship worth developing.  

---

### Atlantic Federation
**Type:** Federation of states  
**Territory:** Atlantic coast from the Amazon delta south through current-day Pará, Maranhão, and the northeastern and southeastern Brazilian coast, ending at the borders of the historic States of São Paulo and Paraná. The Atlantic Forest corridor runs through its interior.  
**Origin:** A conglomerate of states with different backgrounds — native Hesperian, European-descended, and African-descended communities — that formed a federation to resist invasion from southern neighbors. Unity was politically motivated, not culturally homogeneous.  
**Myco-Net character:** The fragmented Atlantic Forest Myco-Net runs through the Federation's interior. Patchy infrastructure — the result of long historical disruption — produces real internal inequality: wealthier, better-connected states versus those still working around degraded substrate corridors.  
**Political character:** The patchiness of its Myco-Net is also a source of political identity. States within the Federation have very different relationships to the Synth-Net, which makes the Federation's Congress votes unpredictable and frequently negotiated internally before they are cast externally.

---

### Kingdom of Chaco
**Type:** Dynastic kingdom  
**Territory:** Current-day Paraguay plus the Brazilian states of Mato Grosso do Sul and Paraná, and the Argentine provinces of Rio Grande, Formosa, Chaco, Corrientes, and Misiones.  
**Origin:** The Chaco region was historically one of the last areas to be incorporated into colonial states because its peoples resisted effectively and the terrain resisted infrastructure. A Chaco-centered expansionist kingdom pushed both north and south before being hemmed in by the Amazonian Treaty — which it was the last polity to sign.  
**Myco-Net character:** Chaco's government controls the Pantanal. The Kingdom manages key transit corridors between the Pantanal's adaptive substrate and the southern grassland networks.  
**Political character:** Traditionally expansionist. Resentful of Treaty constraints. The awkward presence at the Congress table: powerful, never entirely trusted, and always capable of finding a legal argument for why the Treaty shouldn't apply to this particular situation. The "Kingdom" designation — unique among South Hesperian polities — signals a political culture that never fully adopted republican norms.

---

### Eunomia Republic
**Type:** Presidential republic  
**Territory:** The Pampas grasslands south of the Chaco Kingdom's border, running south to the beginning of the Patagonian geographical region.  
**Myco-Net character:** The least Myco-Net-integrated polity in South Hesperia. The fungal substrate thins significantly at southern latitudes and in the grassland biome.  
**Political character:** The Republic takes its name from the Greek goddess Eunomia—both the goddess of "good pastures" and "good laws". Wealthy in a way that does not depend on the Synth-Net — agricultural and pastoral power, not digital infrastructure power. Participates in the Congress because it must, not because the Synth-Net means anything culturally to its population. Simultaneously less vulnerable to Synth-Net attacks and less motivated to fund its defense. Attempted to expand further south before being stopped by the Chaco Kingdom's threat of invasion — which led to the creation of the Aonikenk Protectorate.

---

### Aonikenk Protectorate
**Type:** Congress-administered protectorate  
**Territory:** Patagonia from the Eunomia Republic's southern border through Tierra del Fuego, plus the South Atlantic islands.  
**Origin:** Created by the Hesperian Congress to prevent war — specifically, to stop the Eunomia Republic from expanding south while the Chaco Kingdom threatened to invade the Eunomia if they did. The Aonikenk peoples were made a Congress protectorate as a side effect of someone else's border dispute, not out of genuine concern for their governance needs.  
**Myco-Net character:** Sparse. Southern latitudes and Patagonian terrain do not support the fungal density found in the basin regions. The protectorate has limited Synth-Net integration.  
**Political character:** The Aonikenk and related peoples of Patagonia were historically among the most successful at resisting incorporation — their mobility and the terrain made them difficult to pin down. In this timeline, that mobility remains culturally central. This creates a genuine governance problem for a Congress that thinks in terms of nodes, fixed addresses, and registered participants. The Protectorate's relationship with the Congress is foundationally transactional and often resentful.

## The Hesperian Congress

A continental Decentralized Autonomous Organization that manages collective decisions requiring trans-national coordination. It is not a world government — it governs only Hesperia (the Americas), and even within Hesperia its authority is limited to matters the member polities have agreed to delegate. It was not founded from idealism. It was founded to prevent a war over the Amazon Basin.

Votes are delegated through representatives, segmented primarily by geographical region. The architecture maps onto the Synth-Net's node structure: regional clusters with their own consensus rhythms, connected to the broader network but not subordinate to it in local matters.

---

## The Amazonian Treaty
The founding instrument of the Hesperian Congress. Signed to pause territorial claims over the Amazon Basin and establish it as neutral, Congress-administered territory — the Amazon Commonwealth. Key provisions:
- No autonomous military presence permitted in the Commonwealth
- Scientific, humanitarian, and ecological missions are permitted and managed by the Congress
- Territorial claims are paused, not extinguished — they remain on record
- The Treaty runs for 50-year terms, renewed by Congress vote

The Treaty renewal vote is the single most politically explosive recurring event in Hesperian politics. Every faction has a position. Every renewal is a crisis in miniature.

## The SubDAO Structure
At minimum, the following SubDAOs are established in canon:
- **Security SubDAO** — oversight of Synth-Net threat response, protocols, and the Agents
- **Innovation SubDAO** — research and development funding, experimental protocols

The **Agents Project** was a joint initiative of the Security and Innovation SubDAOs. The Security SubDAO wanted a defensive capability no automated system could provide. The Innovation SubDAO wanted proof of a new category of being. Neither fully informed the main Congress of what they were funding. The main Congress was preoccupied with day-to-day affairs and did not investigate closely — until ROBERT deleted the creation records. The Congress's response (the BYRON reformation) was partly principled governance and partly institutional panic. The distinction is intentionally left unresolved.
